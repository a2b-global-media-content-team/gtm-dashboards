Why standardise to client
The "Client Name" dimension (client) is already registered in GA4. Standardising to client means no new dimension registration — just Tags 138 and 140 updated. After migration, all events filterable with a single client = [name] condition in GA4 Explore and Looker Studio.

!
Do NOT simply rename the parameters — your Looker Studio reports will break If Tags 138 and 140 switch from rn_client_name / client_logo_view to client in one go, Looker Studio reports pointing at the old dimensions stop receiving new data. Switching those reports to client makes historical data invisible. Either way you lose continuity at the switchover date.

Correct approach: dual-parameter transition (v205 → v206, minimum 1 year)

If dual-parameter sending starts today (20 May 2026), the earliest safe removal of rn_client_name and client_logo_view is 20 May 2027. Do not remove old params before that date.

NEW
Banner tracking — full redesign (Tags 166, 175, 205, 207, 219 replaced)

Problems with old setup: (1) Tag 205 read all banners at DOM Ready — counted every banner regardless of whether it was seen, because AdRotate places all rotating banners in the DOM simultaneously. (2) No page location data — article pages produced thousands of unique URLs for what is one banner placement. (3) Homepage news and featured sections were indistinguishable. (4) GA4 custom dimension fp_featured_supplier_image was receiving no data due to a fp_ vs hp_ typo in the parameter name. (5) Click tracking used a different parameter name (banner_img_src) from impression tracking.

New approach: One Custom HTML tag (241) using IntersectionObserver fires once per banner per page load when the banner actually enters the viewport. Handles AdRotate rotation correctly: when a g-dyn div switches from display:none to display:block, the IntersectionObserver detects the child .gofollow element entering the viewport and fires. Deduplication prevents double-counting.

Why Ad Group ID for banner_location: Decoded from the data-track attribute on every .gofollow link. Chosen over URL-pattern matching because: all news/article pages share Ad Group 12 (solves the thousands-of-URLs problem); homepage news (ID 1) and featured (ID 6) are naturally distinct on the same URL; new ad groups are automatically captured without any GTM changes.

Known Ad Group IDs: 1 = homepage news · 6 = homepage featured · 9 = main P&S · 10 = events · 12 = all article pages · 3, 4, 14, 16… = individual P&S pages
NEW
Tag 240 (orphaned ad_impression push) deleted Was pushing a custom ad_impression event to the dataLayer with no matching trigger or GA4 tag — all data silently lost. The new Tag 241 (IntersectionObserver) supersedes this approach entirely. Tag 240 deleted.
NEW
Two new GA4 custom dimensions required banner_location (event scope — Ad Group ID) and page_path (event scope — window.location.pathname). Both sent by the new banner_impression (Tag 242) and banner_click (Tag 243) events. Must be registered in GA4 before importing v204 JSON.
!
DATA INTERRUPTION — banner_img_src ("Client Banner Clicks") — 20 May 2026 The "Client Banner Clicks" GA4 custom dimension (banner_img_src) stopped receiving data when v204 was published on 20 May 2026. Old Tag 219 (which sent banner_img_src) was deleted as part of the banner redesign. New Tag 243 (banner_click) sends ad_rotate_image instead. The equivalent data is now available via the "AdRotate Image" dimension (ad_rotate_image) filtered to banner_click events. Historical data under banner_img_src is preserved in GA4. Any Looker Studio report using "Client Banner Clicks" must be updated to use "AdRotate Image" + event name = banner_click.

Note: fp_featured_supplier_image ("Homepage Featured Supplier src") also goes quiet in v204, but this dimension was already receiving no data before v204 due to a pre-existing parameter name mismatch (fp_ vs hp_ prefix) — no new interruption.
★
RULE — All future GTM-GA4 work: data interruptions must be flagged and confirmed before any change is made Any GTM change that causes a registered GA4 custom dimension to stop receiving data is a data interruption. Before making such a change: (1) identify which registered dimensions are affected and state the exact date data will stop; (2) confirm whether any Looker Studio report uses that dimension; (3) get explicit confirmation to proceed. The v204 banner_img_src interruption above was accepted retrospectively — this must not happen again.
